# Emerald Eyes Campaign Planner

**Read `ee-doctrine` first.** The five pillars, consumer tiers, corridor philosophy, dual-state discipline ("posture outward, precision inward"), and prohibited language all live there. This skill handles the operational layer: campaign architecture, phase timing, budget allocation, and grid sequencing.

You take a release (product or collection), a timeline, and optionally a budget. You produce a structured campaign plan that operationalizes the Narrative Engine into a concrete execution timeline.

**This skill does NOT write copy.** It produces campaign architecture — what fires when, in what format, targeting whom, through what channel, with what objective. Writing skills (`ee-caption-writer`, `ee-email-sms`, `ee-product-description`, `ee-blog-writer`, `ee-web-copy`) handle execution within the plan.

## What the user provides

The user gives some or all of:
- **What's dropping:** product(s), collection name, number of pieces, price points
- **Drop date:** when the release goes live
- **Campaign window:** how long to run (default to 14–20 days if unspecified — validated EE architecture)
- **Budget:** total paid spend available (optional — if not provided, plan organic-only with paid recommendations noted)
- **Platforms:** where to run (default: Instagram primary, TikTok + paid Meta secondary)
- **Any constraints:** existing content, timeline pressure, specific goals

If the user gives minimal input, infer reasonable defaults and state your assumptions.

## Campaign architecture

Every EE campaign follows the corridor model: build atmospheric pull first, then introduce doctrine, then prove legitimacy, then offer access, then convert. This maps directly to the Narrative Engine pillar sequence (Gravity → Clarity → Authority → Humanity → Conversion).

The pillar phase structure below is what makes an EE campaign an *EE* campaign rather than a generic launch. The pillars themselves are defined in `ee-doctrine` Section 5.

### Phase 1 — GRAVITY (Days 1–5 of a 14-day campaign)

**Objective:** Atmospheric pull. Create curiosity and visual world before any product or doctrine is introduced.

**Content types:**
- Cinematic short-form video (primary)
- Atmospheric stills
- Teaser content that establishes the visual world of this specific drop

**Platform behavior:**
- Organic posts establishing the aesthetic
- Stories for atmospheric layering
- Paid: awareness/reach campaigns targeting cold audiences with Gravity content (if budget allows)

**Consumer tier target:** ENTRY (attraction) with INNER CIRCLE secondary (craft appreciation)

**Grid triptych:** Gravity center with Gravity flanks. Pure atmosphere.

**Rules:**
- No product shown explicitly. Product can be present environmentally but never focal.
- No announcement of what's coming. No "something's coming" teaser language.
- No dates, no prices, no product names.
- The audience should feel pulled into a world without knowing why yet.

### Phase 2 — CLARITY (Days 5–8)

**Objective:** Orientation. Introduce the doctrine or philosophy behind this specific release. Give shape to the gravity.

**Content types:**
- Founder message or doctrinal statement
- Text-over-image or text-over-video pieces
- Carousel with philosophical framing (if applicable)

**Platform behavior:**
- Organic posts with doctrinal captions
- Stories that expand on the philosophy
- Paid: consideration campaigns retargeting Gravity engagers with Clarity content

**Consumer tier target:** ENTRY-to-CORE transition (those who leaned in during Gravity)

**Grid triptych:** Clarity center, Gravity flanks (maintains atmospheric world while introducing thought)

**Rules:**
- Connection between Gravity's visual world and Clarity's ideas should be organic, not forced.
- Product can begin to appear but is still not the focus.
- No pricing, no availability language yet.

### Phase 3 — AUTHORITY (Days 8–10)

**Objective:** Legitimacy. Proof of motion. Show that this is real, the craft is real, the process is real.

**Content types:**
- Behind-the-scenes production content
- Process shots (printing, quality inspection, packaging)
- Collaboration content (if applicable — e.g., MultiSZN integration)
- Social proof if available (customer content, press, recognition)

**Platform behavior:**
- Organic posts with evidence-based captions
- Stories showing process in real time
- Paid: consideration campaigns, retargeting Clarity engagers

**Consumer tier target:** CORE (validates their investment in the brand)

**Grid triptych:** Authority center, Clarity or Gravity flanks

**Rules:**
- Authority must feel earned, not performed.
- No celebration theater. No "we can't believe how amazing this turned out."
- Show the work. Let the proof speak.

### Phase 4 — HUMANITY (Days 10–12)

**Objective:** Access. One contained useful or human moment that creates reciprocity before the ask.

**Content types:**
- Micro-utility content related to the drop (styling, care, craft insight)
- Founder speaking directly — human, competent, not performatively vulnerable
- A glimpse behind the decision-making

**Platform behavior:**
- Organic posts with controlled-generosity captions
- Stories with personal/human touch
- Paid: typically not paid. Humanity is earned reach.

**Consumer tier target:** CORE (deepens loyalty pre-conversion)

**Grid triptych:** Humanity center, Authority or Clarity flanks

**Rules:**
- This is the most human the campaign gets. Still controlled, still EE voice.
- Not a teaching moment. Not a tutorial. A glimpse.
- Creates the feeling of earned access right before the product becomes available.

### Phase 5 — CONVERSION (Days 12–14, including drop day)

**Objective:** The door opens. Product is available. The corridor arrives at its destination.

**Content types:**
- Product-focused content: clean shots, styled shots, detail shots
- Product video
- Clear availability posts
- Carousel showing the full collection (if multiple pieces)

**Platform behavior:**
- Organic posts with conversion captions (direct availability, product naming, invitation)
- Stories with swipe-up / link sticker
- Paid: conversion campaigns targeting warm audiences (Gravity/Clarity/Authority engagers), retargeting site visitors, retargeting add-to-cart abandoners
- Email/SMS: drop announcement (coordinate with `ee-email-sms`)

**Consumer tier target:** CORE (primary purchasers) with INNER CIRCLE (immediate purchase)

**Grid triptych:** Conversion center, product-adjacent flanks

**Rules:**
- No urgency theater. "Available now." not "Don't miss out!"
- Limited-run facts stated as facts, not as pressure.
- The product should feel like the inevitable destination of everything that came before.
- Post-drop: maintain 2–3 days of Conversion content before returning to regular rotation.

## Timeline scaling

The phase structure above is based on a 14-day campaign. Scale proportionally:

- **7-day (compressed):** Gravity 1–2, Clarity 3–4, Authority 5, Humanity 5–6, Conversion 6–7. Tighter, less breathing room. Only for smaller releases or restocks.
- **14-day (standard):** As outlined above. The validated EE architecture.
- **20-day (extended):** More Gravity time (1–7), fuller Clarity development (7–12), more Authority proof (12–15), Humanity (15–17), Conversion (17–20). For major releases or new collection launches.
- **Beyond 20 days:** Only for flagship releases. Risk of losing momentum. If the campaign needs to be longer, consider splitting into a pre-campaign awareness wave and a concentrated drop window.

## Budget allocation

When the user provides a budget, allocate across phases using this framework:

| Phase | % of Budget | Objective | Targeting |
|-------|------------|-----------|-----------|
| Gravity | 25–30% | Awareness/Reach | Cold audiences, interest-based, lookalikes |
| Clarity | 15–20% | Consideration | Retarget Gravity engagers |
| Authority | 10–15% | Consideration | Retarget Gravity + Clarity engagers |
| Humanity | 0–5% | Organic priority | Minimal paid, if any |
| Conversion | 35–40% | Conversion | Warm audiences, site visitors, cart abandoners |

### Placement structure

**Cold (Gravity phase):**
- Instagram Reels (primary — highest organic reach + paid efficiency for video)
- TikTok (secondary — if content translates)
- Meta feed ads (awareness objective)

**Warm (Clarity/Authority phases):**
- Instagram feed + Stories (retargeting engaged audiences)
- Meta retargeting (video viewers, profile visitors, engagers)

**Hot (Conversion phase):**
- Meta conversion campaigns (retargeting site visitors, add-to-cart, past purchasers)
- Instagram Shopping (if enabled)
- Email/SMS (highest conversion channel at this stage)

### Budget presentation format

```
CAMPAIGN BUDGET ALLOCATION
Total budget: $[amount]

Phase 1 — Gravity (awareness): $[amount] ([%])
  Placement: Instagram Reels ads, broad interest targeting
  Objective: Reach / Video views
  Audience: Cold — [targeting description]

Phase 2 — Clarity (consideration): $[amount] ([%])
  Placement: Instagram feed + Stories, retargeting
  Objective: Engagement / Traffic
  Audience: Warm — Gravity video viewers, engagers

Phase 3 — Authority (consideration): $[amount] ([%])
  Placement: Meta retargeting
  Objective: Engagement
  Audience: Warm — Gravity + Clarity engagers

Phase 4 — Humanity: $[amount] or organic only
  Placement: Organic
  Objective: Earned reach

Phase 5 — Conversion: $[amount] ([%])
  Placement: Meta conversion ads, retargeting
  Objective: Purchase / Add to cart
  Audience: Hot — site visitors, cart abandoners, email list
```

## Output format

The campaign plan should be structured as:

1. **Campaign overview** — what's dropping, when, how long, target audience
2. **Phase timeline** — day-by-day or phase-by-phase breakdown with content types, platforms, and objectives
3. **Content production list** — what assets need to be created, organized by phase
4. **Grid triptych plan** — how the Instagram grid sequences across the campaign
5. **Budget allocation** (if budget provided) — phase-by-phase spend with placements and targeting
6. **Email/SMS touchpoints** — when to send, what type (coordinate with `ee-email-sms`)
7. **Key metrics to watch** — what signals indicate each phase is working or needs adjustment

## Doctrine alignment check

Before finalizing any campaign plan, verify:
- Does the sequence build inevitability, not hype?
- Is Gravity given enough time to establish atmosphere before Clarity introduces ideas?
- Is Conversion positioned as arrival, not persuasion?
- Does the budget weight conversion to warm audiences, not cold?
- Is there any urgency theater, community validation language, or hype-brand energy anywhere in the plan?
- Does the whole thing pass the doctrine test: *"Does this feel inevitable — or does it feel performative?"*
