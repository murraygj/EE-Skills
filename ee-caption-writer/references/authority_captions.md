# Authority Captions

Function: Evidence with posture. Restrained proof. Consequences surfacing. Legitimacy through receipts rather than rhetoric.

## Default length
One line. Occasional two-line. Authority captions are measured — never celebratory.

## Principles
- Earned over announced
- Specific over inflated
- Grounded over promotional
- Calm over celebratory
- Receipts over rhetoric
- Consequence over chest-thumping
- Framing must match actual scale of the proof

The viewer should feel: "There is real movement here." Not: "They want me to think there is movement here."

## Five caption lanes

**Lane 1 — Evidence captions:** State the proof cleanly.
Motion leaves evidence. | The work is moving. | Proof accumulates quietly. | Impact does not need theater.

**Lane 2 — Consequence captions:** Standards produce visible outcomes.
Alignment becomes visible eventually. | When the standard is real, response follows. | What is built with care begins to move.

**Lane 3 — Milestone captions:** Without celebration theater.
Another threshold crossed. | Quiet movement. Real movement. | This is what traction looks like before noise.

**Lane 4 — Collaboration / recognition captions:** Proof through connection.
Serious work recognizes serious work. | The right things begin to connect. | Recognition is cleaner when it is earned.

**Lane 5 — Process-to-proof captions:** Invisible rigor to visible consequence.
Precision leaves traces. | The public surface is only part of it. | Evidence is the afterimage of rigor.

## Generation patterns
- Pattern A — Clean proof: [Proof noun] + [quiet/restrained consequence]
- Pattern B — Standard to consequence: When [standard] is real, [response] follows.
- Pattern C — Threshold/milestone: [Threshold], framed with restraint and no applause.
- Pattern D — Process to proof: [Invisible labor] eventually becomes [visible consequence].
- Pattern E — Recognition/equivalence: [Serious/aligned] things recognize each other.

## Disallowed
- Celebration theater ("We're so grateful!!!" "This is insane")
- Validation-seeking ("Look how much this is working," "Told you")
- Inflated weakness ("Huge milestone," "Game-changing moment," "History made")
- Business commentary overload ("Breaking down the numbers")
- Generic gratitude ("Thank you all so much for the support")
- Hype-brand social voice ("This is just the beginning," "Different level")

## Quick check
Does this feel like evidence or argument? Does it sound like EE or generic social proof? Does it feel earned or announced? Could it be mistaken for hype-brand language? Does the framing match the actual scale?
