# Clarity Captions

Function: Doctrinal articulation. Articulates perspective, sharpens the idea, gives shape to the doctrine. Makes a real point.

## Default length
One to two lines. Can run slightly longer than Gravity but still compressed.

## Principles
- Declarative over suggestive — make a real point
- Precise over ornamental — sharpened, not embellished
- Philosophical but grounded — tethered to reality and action
- Orientation, not explanation — do not decode the visual
- Reward attention — give a more exact perspective
- Maintain posture — no pandering, no flattening

## Six caption lanes

**Lane 1 — Verdict captions:** Condensed doctrine. Clean, hard, direct.
Vision is not passive. | Clarity does not comfort. It corrects. | Seeing clearly creates obligation. | Alignment is not aesthetic. It is consequence.

**Lane 2 — Reframe captions:** Correct a weak default frame, replace with EE frame.
Discipline is not restriction. It is alignment. | Attention is cheap. Intention is not. | Most drift begins as tolerated imprecision.

**Lane 3 — Calibration captions:** Self-confrontation, reality, refinement.
Misalignment gets expensive once you can see it. | You cannot refine what you refuse to name. | Reality does not bend to preference.

**Lane 4 — Builder / contribution captions:** Action, craft, form.
Build what should exist. | Craft is devotion made visible. | What you refuse matters as much as what you build.

**Lane 5 — Invitation captions:** Selective but not arrogant.
This is for people willing to see fully. | Some things do not need persuasion. Only recognition. | The right people do not need this softened.

**Lane 6 — Posture captions:** Stance, discipline, corridor philosophy.
We are not here to convince. | Clarity over volume. | This is a system, not a mood.

## Generation patterns
- Pattern A — Clean verdict: [Principle] is not [common frame]. It is [EE frame].
- Pattern B — Consequence line: When/If [perception], [responsibility follows].
- Pattern C — Correction line: Most people [weak frame]. [Stronger frame].
- Pattern D — Form line: [Belief/standard] becomes visible through [work/execution].
- Pattern E — Invitation line: [Recognition/alignment] is obvious to [the right people].
- Pattern F — Compression line: [Short declarative.] [Second sentence sharpens.]

## Disallowed
- Too Gravity-coded (atmospheric fragments with no clear idea)
- Too explanatory ("This piece represents the importance of discipline")
- Too motivational ("Chase your dreams," "Be your best self")
- Too superior ("Most people are too blind to understand this")
- Too verbose (long-form mini-essays, stacked metaphors)

## Quick check
Does this make a real point? Does it feel precise, declarative, and grounded? Could it be mistaken for generic motivation? Could it be mistaken for a Gravity caption? Does it leave the viewer with a sharper frame?
