# Conversion Captions

Function: Measured invitation with confidence. The door opening — not someone pulling the viewer through it. Product centrality without commerce panic.

## Default length
One line — often the shortest of any pillar. Occasional two-line. Conversion captions are the most compressed in the entire system.

## Principles
- Invitation over persuasion
- Legibility over hype
- Posture over prompting
- Specificity over noise
- Availability over pressure
- Product centrality without commerce panic

The viewer should feel: "This is available." Not: "They are trying to get me to buy right now."

## Five caption lanes

**Lane 1 — Direct availability captions:** Clarity is the priority.
Available now. | Now available. | Online now. | The release is live.

**Lane 2 — Product naming captions:** Direct product stills or cleaner product-led pieces.
The Cross Hoodie. | The Double E Hoodie. | The release. | The artifact.

**Lane 3 — Invitation captions:** Softer than direct CTA, still conversion-capable.
Enter here. | Invitation remains open. | For those already inside the frame. | The artifact is available.

**Lane 4 — Product-world captions:** Connect product to the corridor without overexplaining.
Built to be entered, not explained. | Worn as intended. | The corridor leads somewhere. | The product is where the world becomes tangible.

**Lane 5 — Selective literal CTA captions:** Used sparingly and precisely.
Shop now. | Explore the release. | Enter the release. | Available on-site now.

## Generation patterns
- Pattern A — Direct availability: [Availability statement]. Use when the piece is already highly legible.
- Pattern B — Product naming: [Product name / artifact identifier]. When the image is strong and naming creates enough weight.
- Pattern C — Invitation: [Invitation phrase] + [contained entry language]. Softer posture, audience already warmed by corridor.
- Pattern D — Product-to-world: [Artifact] as natural extension of [world/standard/corridor]. Preserves corridor language.
- Pattern E — Selective literal CTA: [Literal CTA] in compressed EE tone. Used sparingly in paid or conversion-focused contexts.

## Disallowed
- Urgency theater ("Hurry before it's gone," "Don't miss out," "Last chance," "Selling fast")
- Generic brand CTA voice ("Link in bio," "Tap in now," "Go cop," "Grab yours today")
- Price-forward framing ("Only $170," "Affordable luxury")
- Hype-commerce language ("Biggest drop yet," "This one is crazy," "Y'all asked, we delivered")
- Overexplained product copy (long feature paragraphs, material breakdowns in caption form)
- Scarcity cosplay ("Almost gone," "Flying off the shelves," "Once it's gone it's gone")

## Quick check
Does this feel like an invitation or a sales pitch? Does it sound like EE or generic commerce? Does it preserve posture while clarifying access? Could it be mistaken for urgency theater? Is the framing proportional to what is actually being offered?
