# Gravity Captions

Function: Interpretive anchor. Frames the viewer's posture. Tells them the work should be read, not merely consumed. Creates pressure, implication, and orientation without resolving meaning.

## Default length
One short line. Occasional two-line captions used sparingly.

## Principles
- Minimal language
- Interpretive, not explanatory — never decode the imagery
- Weighted tone — deliberate, high-agency, restrained, slightly charged
- Atmospheric congruence — must belong to the same world as the piece
- Compression over cleverness

## Five caption lanes

**Lane 1 — Interpretive anchors:** Tell the viewer how to approach the piece.
Nothing here is accidental. | This requires reading. | Every frame is a decision. | Watch twice. | Look closer.

**Lane 2 — Doctrinal fragments:** Compressed lines that feel extracted from a larger worldview. Short enough to remain atmospheric.
Vision is not passive. | Precision removes excuse. | Consequence is real. | Restraint carries force.

**Lane 3 — Threshold / invitation lines:** The viewer is at the entrance to something.
Some invitations arrive as fire. | Not everyone keeps walking. | The entrance is narrow by design. | Some things are found, not announced.

**Lane 4 — Consequence lines:** Work well for fire, darkness, force, motion, transformation.
Not everything burns by accident. | Force does not always announce itself. | Some thresholds do not reopen. | What changes you rarely asks permission.

**Lane 5 — Recognition / identity anchors:** For a specific kind of mind, without arrogance.
Most people won't see it. | Recognition is enough. | Those who know will stay. | Depth filters on its own.

## Generation patterns
- Pattern A — Structural signal: [This/Nothing/Every frame] + [hidden order or intention]
- Pattern B — Threshold line: [Some/Not everyone/The right people] + [threshold language]
- Pattern C — Consequence fragment: [Force/fire/silence/structure] + [implied consequence]
- Pattern D — Recognition line: [If/Those who/Most people] + [selective perception]
- Pattern E — Rewatch anchor: [Look/Watch/Read] + [deeper-viewing directive]

## Disallowed
- Explanatory ("This piece represents transformation")
- Clarity-coded (too direct for Gravity — save full doctrine articulation for Clarity)
- Generic motivational ("Keep going," "Trust the process")
- Casual/internet-native ("Say less," "Mood," "For the real ones")
- Overwritten/pseudo-poetic (stacked abstraction without force)
- Superior/condescending ("Most people are too shallow for this")

## Quick check
Does this intensify curiosity without explaining? Does it sound like EE, not generic art-brand? Is it compressed and weighted? Could it be mistaken for Clarity or motivation? Does it frame the viewer's posture?
