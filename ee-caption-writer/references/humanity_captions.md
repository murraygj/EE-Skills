# Humanity Captions

Function: Controlled generosity with intelligence. A glimpse of competence. One contained useful thing without changing the brand's identity.

## Default length
One line. Occasional two-line. Never mini-essays.

## Principles
- Glimpse over dump
- Precision over abundance
- Competence over teaching posture
- Human over performatively intimate
- Useful over explanatory
- Calm over chatty

The viewer should feel: "I got something real from that." Not: "This person is trying to teach me."

## Five caption lanes

**Lane 1 — Correction captions:** Identify a common mistake, sharpen perception.
Remove one thing before you add another. | Most people are solving the wrong problem first. | Most technical problems begin as perceptual ones.

**Lane 2 — Reframe captions:** Reorient understanding of a discipline or problem.
Clarity usually comes before motivation. | Taste is often subtraction under pressure. | What you leave out is part of the standard.

**Lane 3 — Micro-utility captions:** One small practical move without expanding into steps.
Lower the light before you add another source. | Cut one unnecessary element first. | Start by fixing the read, not the detail.

**Lane 4 — Perception captions:** Seeing, judgment, calibration over tactics.
A better frame usually starts with better judgment. | Most people don't need more effort. They need better seeing. | Most clutter starts as uncertainty.

**Lane 5 — Controlled generosity captions:** Explicit "glimpse" posture.
Here's something worth keeping. | Small correction. Better result. | Not the whole map. Just something useful.

## Generation patterns
- Pattern A — Direct correction: [Common mistake] + [concise correction]
- Pattern B — Reframe: [Thing people assume] is usually [deeper thing]
- Pattern C — Utility glimpse: [One concise action], no steps
- Pattern D — Standard-to-action: [Standard/restraint] determines [result]
- Pattern E — Controlled generosity: [Small offering] + [contained useful point]

## Disallowed
- Teaching-brand framing ("Here are 5 tips," "Full breakdown below")
- Guru/coach voice ("You need to master this," "The secret nobody tells you")
- Generic value-content voice ("Free game," "Dropping knowledge")
- Performative vulnerability ("Just wanted to be honest with you guys")
- Overfriendly/overfamiliar ("Hope this helps y'all," "Love you guys")
- Overexplained captions (multi-sentence mini-essays)

## Quick check
Does this feel like a glimpse or a lesson? Does it sound like EE or generic creator content? Does it feel human without becoming overfamiliar? Could it be mistaken for coaching language? Is it one contained point, not a framework?
