# Emerald Eyes Doctrine

This is the upstream reference for all Emerald Eyes work. Every other ee-* skill executes against this document. When the doctrine says one thing and a format-specific skill says another, the doctrine wins.

The doctrine has six layers. They are ordered by frequency of use, not by importance. The bottom layers (visual standards, internal engine) are usually consulted only when explicitly relevant; the top layers (the test, voice rules, pillars) apply to virtually every task.

---

## 1. The single test

Before any execution ships, ask one question:

> **"Does this feel inevitable — or does it feel performative?"**

If performative, it doesn't ship. Rewrite, recompose, or kill the piece.

This test governs everything below. If a piece passes every other rule but fails this test, it still fails. If a piece breaks a rule but passes this test deliberately and intentionally, that may be a Creative Override (see section 7).

Companion test for over-explanation:

> **"If you are over-explaining Emerald Eyes, you are compensating for unclear execution."**

When the work is precise, it does not require persuasion. It requires presence. **If copy must decode the visual, the visual is incomplete.**

## 2. Voice rules (apply to all written output)

### Verbatim core

These three lines are foundational and should be preserved exactly when used:

> Emerald Eyes exists because vision demands to be lived out, not admired.
>
> Emerald Eyes is the discipline of learning to see.
>
> For those who recognize discipline as devotion, and contribution as the choice to act.

### Tone

- Declarative
- High-agency
- Minimal
- Controlled intensity

### Sentence compression

- Target sentence length: **8–14 words average**.
- Short sentences create authority. Longer sentences are permitted only when compression would sacrifice clarity.
- Avoid multi-clause motivational phrasing, excess qualifiers, emotional padding.

### Permitted language

- Declarative statements
- Consequence-based framing (what wearing/seeing/doing this signals, not how it makes you feel)
- Symbolic language from the EE lexicon: perception, alignment, discipline, devotion, clarity, structure, consequence, precision, calibration, refusal, standard, inevitability
- Specificity about materials, construction, design decisions
- Stating limitations (run size, hand-produced, inspected) as facts, not as urgency

### Prohibited language

These are hard rejections. None of the following may appear in any EE output regardless of format:

- **Motivational phrasing.** "Trust the journey," "believe in yourself," "chase your dreams," "your potential has no ceiling," "you're unstoppable," "sharpen your edge," "trust the process."
- **Community validation language.** "Join the movement," "be part of something," "belonging to something bigger," "the EE family," "we're all in this together." EE is consequence-based, not identity-forward. The brand establishes a standard and allows alignment to occur. It does not recruit.
- **Urgency/scarcity theater.** "Don't miss out," "limited time only," "act now," "when they're gone, they're gone," "selling fast," "last chance," "hurry." If something is limited, state it as fact. Theater destroys posture.
- **Second-person emotional projection.** "It makes you feel confident," "you'll feel unstoppable," "it changes how you carry yourself." Never tell the reader what to feel.
- **"We" recruiting language.** "We" implies community membership. EE does not have a community. It has a standard.
- **Hype-brand energy.** ALL CAPS for emphasis, excess exclamation marks, multiple emoji, "OMG," "we are SO excited."
- **Generic e-commerce voice.** "Welcome to our store!", "Check out our latest drop!", "Discover...", "Explore..."
- **Casual internet-native voice.** "For the real ones," "say less," "mood," "no caption needed," "iykyk," "if you know you know" (this one is borderline — see Caption Framing below).
- **Explanatory metaphors.** If the symbolism needs to be decoded in copy, the design is incomplete.
- **Capitalized keyword stuffing.** Never capitalize keywords mid-sentence for emphasis ("Black hoodie" not "Black Hoodie" unless it's a proper product name).

### The voice distinction in practice

**Wrong (pre-doctrine):**
> "When you wear Emerald Eyes, you're wearing a belief system — that individuality matters, and that limits are meant to be broken."

**Right (doctrine-aligned):**
> "The garment is not the point. The standard it represents is. Clothing either confirms alignment or it decorates absence. Emerald Eyes does not decorate."

**Wrong:**
> "It's about what it reminds you of — discipline, creativity, individuality."

**Right:**
> "Discipline is visible. It shows in what was chosen and what was refused. Every design decision in this piece is both."

The wrong version tells the reader what to feel. The right version states what is true and lets the reader locate themselves within it.

## 3. The three foundational truths

The doctrine is built on three propositions. Reference these when a piece needs philosophical grounding or when evaluating whether a concept is doctrine-compatible.

**1. Vision is not passive.**
Seeing clearly creates obligation. Attention carries responsibility. What you choose to build, wear, and stand behind becomes an extension of recognition.

**2. Creation is response, not decoration.**
The work exists to direct force, not to signal taste.

**3. Nothing is original — everything is a configuration of inspiration.**
Meaning emerges through judgment, restraint, and integration. EE is not self-expression. It is self-alignment through creation.

These three truths govern how EE thinks about influence, reference, originality, and craft. They are why EE never claims novelty and never apologizes for influence — what matters is the configuration, not the components.

---

## 4. Brand posture

The two non-negotiable posture rules:

> **Emerald Eyes is consequence-based, not identity-forward.**
> We do not ask who the customer is. We establish a standard and allow alignment to occur.

> **Luxury is not inclusion. Luxury is precision + refusal.**

Practical consequences of consequence-based posture:

- Never write "for people who..." or "designed for..." or "made for..." Don't define the audience explicitly. Establish the standard and let alignment occur.
- Never recruit. The brand is not seeking members. It is producing artifacts and articulating standards.
- Never apologize for who EE isn't for. "Those unwilling to engage deeply are not the wrong audience — they are simply not the audience."
- Limitations are facts, not exclusivity theater. "Limited to 100 pieces" is a statement of production reality. "ONLY 100 LEFT — GET YOURS NOW" is theater.

## 5. The Narrative Engine (five pillars)

EE content rotates through five expressions. Every piece of content (post, video, image, caption, email, page) serves one primary pillar. The pillar determines voice register, function, and target tier.

The sequence builds the corridor: Gravity attracts, Clarity orients, Authority validates, Humanity earns reciprocity, Conversion invites.

### I. GRAVITY — Mood Pieces
**Function:** Attraction.
**Creates:** Atmosphere before explanation.
**Characteristics:** Cinematic, symbolic, minimal language, high aesthetic control, emotional but restrained.
**Purpose:** Establish depth before clarity. Signal taste before instruction.
**What it does NOT do:** Teach, sell, or explain.

This is where Emerald Eyes feels inevitable.

### II. CLARITY — Doctrine Pieces
**Function:** Orientation.
**Creates:** Articulation of principles, standards, beliefs, frames.
**Characteristics:** Precise language, declarative tone, structured thought, philosophical but grounded.
**Purpose:** Give shape to the gravity. Reward those who leaned in.

### III. AUTHORITY — Social Proof Pieces
**Function:** Legitimacy.
**Creates:** Proof of motion, proof of demand, proof of impact.
**Examples:** Behind-the-scenes production, collaborations, testimonials in identity language, metrics (subtly), features, milestones.
**Purpose:** Demonstrate that the doctrine produces results.

Authority should feel earned, not asked.

### IV. HUMANITY — Micro-Utility Pieces
**Function:** Access.
**Creates:** Small, actionable insights that improve perception, discipline, or craft — without turning EE into a teaching brand.
**Examples:**
- "Stop over-lighting your shots. Shadow is structure."
- "Most people confuse motivation with clarity."
- "If you're designing something, remove one unnecessary element."
- "Your standard is the ceiling of your identity."

**Characteristics:** Short. Precise. Tactical but minimal.
**Purpose:** Humanize. Demonstrate competence. Create reciprocity. Build loyalty without diluting mystique.

Micro-utility says: "I know what I'm doing. Here's a glimpse." It is generosity without overexposure.

### V. CONVERSION — Product Pieces
**Function:** Invitation.
**Not:** Loud selling. Urgency spam.
**Characteristics:** Clean product showcases, worn in real environments, minimal copy, strong posture.
**Purpose:** Product feels like a natural extension of gravity + doctrine, not a departure.

## 6. Consumer tiers (behavioral profiles)

Three tiers. Identified by behavior, not demographics. Each tier responds to different pillars and breaks for different reasons.

**Full behavioral profiles in `references/behavioral-profiles.md`** — consult that file when targeting a specific tier or evaluating whether a piece will land. The summary below is for quick recall.

### Entry — "The Gravitational Pull"
- Feels misaligned, can't articulate why
- Engages with aesthetic first
- Passive-receptive: watches, saves, occasionally likes
- Captured by Gravity, leaks at Conversion-too-early
- Needs 5-8 touchpoints before active engagement
- **Drop-off triggers:** urgency framing, over-explanation, community validation language, inconsistency in atmospheric quality

### Core — "The Doctrinal Resonance"
- Resonates with doctrine
- Uses EE language patterns back ("alignment," "discipline," "seeing")
- Saves Clarity pieces at higher rate than Gravity (the inversion signal)
- Multi-page browsing, repeat purchase within 60-90 days
- Sensitive to hypocrisy — calibrate against the stated standard
- **Drop-off triggers:** quality regression, over-commercialization, lack of depth progression, engagement without reciprocity

### Inner Circle — "The Living Standard"
- Lives the philosophy
- Selective high-signal engagement, substantive comments
- Generates unsolicited content featuring EE
- Purchases immediately on drop
- May propose collaboration or contribution
- **Drop-off triggers:** perceived sellout, loss of creative ambition, scaling that degrades craft, founder absence from the work

### Targeting principle

Match the pillar to the tier where that pillar has highest activation:

| Pillar | Primary tier | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Gravity | Entry | Inner Circle (craft appreciation) |
| Clarity | Core | Entry-to-Core transition |
| Authority | Core | Inner Circle |
| Humanity | Core | Entry (if authentic) |
| Conversion | Core | Inner Circle (already arrived) |

**Never target Conversion at Entry. The corridor isn't ready. They leave.**

## 7. The Corridor (and what's underneath it)

### The corridor

EE does not have a funnel. It has a corridor.

> Wide at the entrance. Narrow by design.
> Most people will turn around. The right ones keep walking.

A corridor is engineered:
- Entry points are deliberate
- Lighting is controlled
- Direction is intentional
- Friction is minimized

The corridor is the metaphor that replaces the funnel. A funnel implies conversion math. A corridor implies posture changes at each stage — visitors adopt a different stance as they go deeper.

### Dual-state discipline

The corridor is *philosophical* in design and *engineered* in operation. This is the most important hidden mechanic.

> **Posture outward. Precision inward.**
> Emerald Eyes maintains composure in public.
> It maintains pressure in private.

Externally: inevitability. Internally: optimization.

- Traffic is segmented
- Audiences are profiled
- Funnels are structured
- Creative is tested
- Drop-off points are diagnosed
- Conversion pathways are tightened

"Luxury stands still long enough to be found" does NOT mean "Luxury does not optimize." Posture is aesthetic. Optimization is operational. One governs perception. The other governs performance.

**Emerald Eyes does not beg. It also does not guess.**

This matters for Claude: when planning campaigns, allocating budget, or making any operational decision, do not confuse the doctrine's outward restraint with internal passivity. The campaign math is ruthless even when the brand voice is patient.

## 8. Interpretive anchors (how to prime depth without explaining)

Anchors tell the viewer how to process what they're seeing. They reduce ambiguity without reducing depth. They do not explain the art — they frame it.

### Linguistic anchors
A single line that frames the viewing posture.

> "Nothing here is accidental."
> "Most people won't see it."
> "Every frame is a decision."
> "This is about inevitability."

### Conceptual anchors
A theme stated before a piece, so the audience maps imagery onto it.

> Before a symbolic video: "Discipline is not control. It's alignment."

Now when they watch (fire, columns, exhale, vortex), they map imagery onto "alignment." You have provided a lens.

### Structural anchors
Consistency across pieces. If every Gravity piece opens with silence, uses the same typography, ends with a doctrinal fragment — structure becomes the anchor. Repetition trains interpretation.

### Identity anchors
Signals that this is for a specific type of mind.

> "If you recognize it, you recognize it."

Invites deeper engagement. Filters casual consumption. The viewer wants to prove they "get it."

### Symbol recurrence anchors
Repeated motifs (eyes, fire, crosses, columns, smoke, palms, butterflies, screens) stop being random visuals over time. They become language. Recurring symbols are anchors through accumulation.

## 9. Visual standards (compressed reference)

Full standards are in the doctrine; this is what most often affects writing-skill work (image briefs, captions referencing visuals, web copy that pairs with imagery).

### The symbolic vocabulary
Core symbols and what they represent:
- **Eyes** — perception, witness, the act of seeing
- **Fire** — consequence, devotion, transformation
- **Crosses** — structure, conviction, purpose
- **Isolation** — clarity through removal, signal-to-noise
- **Smoke** — process, atmosphere, what's between states
- **Columns/Cathedrals** — structural inevitability, weight
- **Cars/Bikes** — kinetic discipline, direction
- **Palms/Flowers** — life within structure
- **Screens** — perception mediated, attention
- **Butterflies** — transformation, ephemeral form

Symbols are language. Use them with discipline:
- Minimal competing metaphors
- Maximum 2 symbolic elements per frame
- 1 dominant metaphor per composition
- Repetition of core iconography builds reverence

### Atmosphere
- Contrast-driven, not exposure-balanced
- Controlled in saturation, never chaotic
- Hierarchical in light structure, never flat
- Intentional in tone, never casual

The viewer should feel like they've stepped into a world, not scrolled past a post.

### Density
- Minimum 30% negative space per frame
- If the viewer can't identify the focal subject within 2 seconds, reduce elements
- **Authority increases as density decreases.**

### The mark (intertwined E's)
The mark is sacred. Repetition builds recognition. Restraint builds reverence. The mark should feel ritualized, not experimental.

### Full visual standards
Contrast ratios, motion pacing, typography hierarchy, effects/depth control — see the doctrine source document. Reference when working with the production pipeline skills (`cinematic-prompt-writer`, etc.).

## 10. Creative Override Clause

Discipline includes knowing when to deviate.

The Creative Director (Founder) retains authority to override these standards under specific conditions.

### When override is permitted

An override is valid only if ALL of the following are true:
- The deviation increases emotional precision
- The deviation strengthens narrative impact
- The deviation serves long-term brand authority
- The deviation is intentional, not compensatory

If a rule is broken due to lack of clarity or execution weakness, **it is not an override. It is an error.**

### Acceptable override scenarios

- Increasing density to create deliberate psychological tension
- Collapsing contrast for a moment of conceptual suffocation
- Allowing chaotic motion to convey rupture
- Using ornamental typography to subvert tone intentionally
- Breaking pacing standards to create shock or destabilization

The key condition: **the break must feel deliberate and controlled.**

### Override documentation

When overriding, define:
- Which rule is being broken
- Why it is being broken
- What effect it is intended to produce
- Why adherence would weaken the piece

### Frequency limit

Override use should not exceed **10-15% of total released content per quarter**. If deviation becomes standard, the rule must be rewritten.

### How Claude should use this

When the user requests something that breaks a doctrine rule, Claude should:
1. Note which rule is being broken.
2. Ask whether this is a deliberate Creative Override or whether the user is unaware of the rule.
3. If override: proceed with documentation (briefly note in the output what rule was broken and why).
4. If unaware: explain the rule and let the user decide whether to override or revise.

Never override silently. Never override to be agreeable. The override clause exists for *deliberate* artistic deviation, not for accommodation.

## 11. Closing standard

The doctrine ends with this. It is the test by which everything else should be measured:

> Every mark. Every frame. Every sentence. Every release.
>
> Governed by contrast.
> Guided by restraint.
> Refined through data.
>
> The posture is deliberate.
> The engine is relentless.
>
> If an execution does not meet the standard, it does not ship.
> If a standard weakens the brand, it is rewritten — not ignored.
