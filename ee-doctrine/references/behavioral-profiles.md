# EE Behavioral Signal Profiles

This is the operational targeting layer that sits beneath the three consumer tiers in the main doctrine (Section 6). It translates philosophical alignment into detectable behavioral signals.

**This is not a persona document.** EE does not profile demographics. This document profiles behavioral patterns, engagement signals, and conversion pathways.

When `ee-doctrine` Section 6 gives you the summary, this file gives you the full operational map. Consult it when:
- Writing for a specific tier (the drop-off triggers tell you what NOT to do)
- Planning a campaign and deciding which pillar fires at which tier
- Evaluating whether a piece will reach its intended audience
- Diagnosing why something didn't land (corridor leak vs. tier mismatch)

---

## Doctrine alignment

> "We do not ask who the customer is. We establish a standard and allow alignment to occur."

This document operationalizes HOW alignment is detected, measured, and responded to. It does not change what EE says or shows. It ensures what EE says and shows reaches the right eyes at the right stage.

## Tier 1: ENTRY — "The Gravitational Pull"

### Doctrine alignment
- Feels misaligned but cannot articulate why.
- Attracted to Gravity pieces.
- Engages with aesthetic first.

### Behavioral signals
- Discovers EE through mood/atmosphere content (Gravity pieces, cinematic reels, symbolic imagery).
- Engagement pattern is passive-receptive: watches, saves, occasionally likes. Rarely comments.
- Dwell time on Gravity content is high relative to other content types.
- Low click-through on product or doctrine content at this stage.
- May follow after 2-4 Gravity exposures. Does not follow after a single piece.
- Shares content to close circles (DMs, stories with limited audience) rather than public reshares.
- Responds to atmospheric cues: color grading, pacing, music, visual tension.
- Does NOT respond to declarative messaging, philosophical framing, or explicit brand language at this stage.

### Pillar activation strength (Entry)
1. **Gravity** — primary attractor. This is where Entry consumers are captured.
2. **Authority** — secondary. Behind-the-scenes and collaboration content creates curiosity.
3. **Humanity** — tertiary. Micro-utility pieces can create unexpected pull if they feel authentic.
4. **Clarity** — low activation. Doctrine pieces feel inaccessible at this stage.
5. **Conversion** — lowest activation. Product pieces feel premature without accumulated context.

### Engagement path to next tier
- Requires 5-8 content touchpoints before transitioning from passive consumption to active engagement.
- **Transition signal:** first comment, first story reshare, first click on a Clarity or Humanity piece.
- **Transition accelerators:** a Gravity piece paired with a Conceptual Anchor — gives them a lens that shifts them from feeling to interpreting.
- **Transition friction:** too much Conversion content too early. Product pieces before sufficient Gravity/Clarity exposure breaks the corridor. They leave.

### Drop-off triggers (what NOT to do when writing for Entry)
- Urgency framing in any form (limited time, scarcity language, countdown pressure).
- Over-explanation of brand philosophy in captions. If they have to read a manifesto before the visual lands, the visual failed.
- Inconsistency in atmospheric quality. One low-craft piece breaks accumulated gravity.
- Community validation language ("join the movement," "be part of something"). This signals inclusion-seeking, which contradicts the corridor model.

### Retargeting logic
- Retarget with Gravity content exclusively until transition signal is detected.
- After transition signal: introduce one Clarity piece per three Gravity pieces.
- Do not serve Conversion content to Entry-tier behavioral patterns. The corridor is not ready.

### Platform behavior
- **Discovery:** Instagram Explore, TikTok For You, Pinterest (visual search).
- **Engagement:** Instagram (saves, story views, DMs).
- Entry consumers do not visit the website unprompted. They are not yet searching for the brand.

## Tier 2: CORE — "The Doctrinal Resonance"

### Doctrine alignment
- Resonates with doctrine.
- Responds to Clarity + Micro-Utility.
- Sees self-refinement as identity.

### Behavioral signals
- Engages with Clarity and Humanity content with higher frequency than Gravity alone.
- Comments reflect internalization: they use EE language patterns back ("alignment," "discipline," "seeing").
- **Saves Clarity pieces at a higher rate than Gravity pieces.** This is the inversion signal — they have moved from aesthetic pull to philosophical resonance.
- Clicks through to website. Browses multiple pages per session. Reads the About/Doctrine page.
- Purchases with intentionality: researches the piece, reads product descriptions fully, may visit product page 2-3 times before purchasing.
- Repeat purchase behavior within 60-90 days of first purchase.
- Shares content publicly with personal commentary attached. The share is not passive — it is an identity signal.
- Engages with behind-the-scenes and process content (Authority pieces) because they value craft and rigor.
- Responds to Micro-Utility pieces with high save rates. These feel like earned access.

### Pillar activation strength (Core)
1. **Clarity** — primary resonance driver. Doctrine pieces confirm what they already sense.
2. **Humanity** — strong secondary. Micro-utility creates reciprocity and loyalty.
3. **Authority** — validates their investment. Proof of motion matters at this stage.
4. **Gravity** — still activates but now functions as reinforcement, not discovery.
5. **Conversion** — receptive. Product pieces feel like natural extensions of the doctrine.

### Engagement path to next tier
- Requires sustained engagement over 3-6 months with consistent depth signals.
- **Transition signal:** unsolicited content creation (wearing the brand without being asked), direct messages about the philosophy rather than the product, attempts to collaborate or contribute.
- **Transition accelerators:** Authority pieces featuring real collaborators — this models what Inner Circle participation looks like.
- **Transition friction:** perceived inconsistency between doctrine and execution. Core consumers are the most sensitive to hypocrisy. They are invested enough to be disappointed.

### Drop-off triggers (what NOT to do when writing for Core)
- Quality regression in product or content. Core consumers calibrate against the stated standard.
- Over-commercialization of the feed. If Conversion frequency exceeds the narrative engine rotation, the corridor feels like a funnel. They notice.
- Lack of depth progression. If content stays at the same philosophical level for too long, they plateau and disengage. The doctrine promises continuous calibration — content must model that.
- Engagement without reciprocity. If Core consumers feel like they are speaking into a void (commenting, sharing, receiving no signal back), loyalty erodes.

### Retargeting logic
- Serve a balanced rotation: Clarity and Humanity as primary, Gravity and Authority as secondary, Conversion as punctuation.
- Conversion content is appropriate but must follow the narrative engine sequence — never isolated from the pillar rotation.
- Retarget with new Clarity content within 48 hours of a Clarity save. The save is an intent signal — follow it.
- After first purchase: Authority content (proof of motion, behind-the-scenes of what they now own) reinforces the decision.

### Platform behavior
- **Engagement:** Instagram (comments, saves, shares with commentary), website (multi-page sessions, return visits).
- **Conversion:** website (direct), Instagram Shopping (if enabled), Shopify.
- Core consumers search for the brand by name. Organic search and direct traffic increase at this tier.
- Email list subscribers. They opt in because they want continued access to doctrine and drops.

## Tier 3: INNER CIRCLE — "The Living Standard"

### Doctrine alignment
- Lives the philosophy.
- Builds in their own lane.
- Understands symbolism without anchors.

### Behavioral signals
- Engagement is selective and high-signal. Does not engage with every post — engages deeply with specific pieces.
- Comments are substantive. Not affirmation ("fire" / "hard") but interpretation ("the column framing in the second shot reads as structural inevitability"). They see the architecture.
- Generates original content featuring EE without prompting. The brand appears in their world as a natural element, not a performance.
- Purchases immediately on drop. Does not need the 2-3 visit consideration window. Decision is pre-made by accumulated alignment.
- May purchase multiples or gift pieces to others they identify as aligned.
- Engages with the brand through direct communication channels (DMs, email replies) with substantive observations or contributions.
- Refers others through personal recommendation, not public broadcasting. The corridor model is understood intuitively — they send specific people, not audiences.
- May propose collaboration, contribution, or creative partnership. This is not clout-seeking — it is an offer to participate in the standard.

### Pillar activation strength (Inner Circle)
1. **Authority** — proof of motion and collaboration content resonates most because they see themselves in it.
2. **Gravity** — appreciated at the craft level. They evaluate technique, composition, symbolic density.
3. **Clarity** — still activates but they have internalized the doctrine. New Clarity pieces confirm rather than reveal.
4. **Humanity** — valued for the competence signal. They respect the craft behind the glimpse.
5. **Conversion** — product drops are events. They participate in the ritual, not the transaction.

### Engagement path
- There is no tier beyond this. Inner Circle is the living practice.
- Growth at this tier is measured by depth of contribution and alignment, not by behavioral escalation.
- The goal is not to "convert" Inner Circle members further — it is to maintain the environment that sustains their participation.

### Drop-off triggers (what NOT to do for Inner Circle)
- Perceived sellout or compromise. Inner Circle members hold the brand to its own stated standard more rigorously than anyone.
- Loss of creative ambition. If content becomes formulaic or predictable, the discipline-to-deviation ratio (Creative Override) feels mechanical rather than alive.
- Scaling without maintaining craft standard. Growth that degrades precision is immediately visible to this tier.
- Founder absence from the work. Inner Circle members are aligned with the Creative Director's vision specifically. If the founder's hand becomes invisible, the signal weakens.

### Retargeting logic
- Minimal retargeting needed. Inner Circle members seek the brand actively.
- When used: Authority and Gravity content as primary, timed to drop windows and campaign launches.
- Email is the highest-value channel at this tier. Direct, substantive, low-frequency communication.
- Do not retarget with urgency or scarcity framing under any circumstances. The corridor model means Inner Circle members arrive when they arrive. **Pressure breaks trust permanently at this tier.**

### Platform behavior
- **Engagement:** Instagram (selective, substantive), direct communication (DMs, email).
- **Conversion:** website (direct traffic, bookmarked), email (drop notifications).
- Inner Circle members do not discover the brand through paid channels. They arrived through the corridor over time.
- May be invisible in analytics (low-frequency engagement) but disproportionately high in LTV and referral quality.

## Operational integration

### Corridor positions (the psychological stage map)

Every piece sits at one of four corridor positions:

- **ATTRACTION** — pre-engagement, atmospheric pull (Entry default)
- **ORIENTATION** — beginning to interpret, seeking framework (Entry-to-Core transition)
- **RESONANCE** — internalized doctrine, active engagement (Core default)
- **PARTICIPATION** — contributing, creating, collaborating (Inner Circle default)

When briefing a piece, name the corridor position. It tells Claude what posture the writing should adopt and what assumptions the reader is bringing.

### Targeting matrix

| Pillar | Primary target | Secondary | Never target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gravity | Entry | Inner Circle (craft appreciation) | — |
| Clarity | Core | Entry-to-Core transition | — |
| Authority | Core | Inner Circle | — |
| Humanity | Core | Entry (if authentic) | — |
| Conversion | Core | Inner Circle (already arrived) | **Entry** |

The single non-negotiable rule: **Conversion content does not target Entry.** Serving product pieces to behavioral patterns that haven't progressed past Gravity breaks the corridor. They leave.

### Healthy corridor distribution

A well-functioning corridor produces roughly this audience distribution:
- ~60% Entry
- ~30% Core
- ~10% Inner Circle

Transition rates worth watching (over rolling cohorts):
- Entry → Core: target 15-25% over 6 months
- Core → Inner Circle: target 5-10% over 12 months

If the distribution skews heavy at Entry with low transition, the corridor is leaking — Clarity and Humanity content is failing to convert atmospheric interest into doctrinal engagement.

If the distribution is heavy at Core with low Inner Circle conversion, the Authority pillar may be underrepresented or insufficiently inviting participation.

### Corridor efficiency states

When evaluating whether a piece is working:
- **ALIGNED** — The piece reached its intended tier with expected behavioral signals.
- **LEAKING** — The piece attracted engagement from a lower tier than intended (e.g., a Clarity piece getting only aesthetic saves instead of doctrinal engagement). Suggests the corridor has gaps.
- **OVER-INDEXING** — The piece activated a higher tier than intended. Not necessarily negative — may indicate the piece should be reclassified or the tier boundary recalibrated.
